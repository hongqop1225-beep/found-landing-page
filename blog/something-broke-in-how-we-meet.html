Something broke in how we meet people.

Dating apps gave us endless choices and somehow made it harder to choose anyone.

Swipe left. Swipe left. Swipe left.

That's what the last decade of dating has sounded like for most of us. A thumb, a screen, a quiet exhaustion that didn't used to be there.

We were promised variety. We got overwhelm. We were promised connection. We got content.

The dating app became the thing we did instead of dating.

The paradox of choice, but for people

Barry Schwartz wrote about the paradox of choice almost twenty years ago — the idea that more options don't make us happier, they make us anxious. It turns out that's doubly true when the "options" are human beings.

When you have 40 matches in your inbox, each conversation feels disposable. Why try hard with one person when tomorrow there might be someone better?

And so we don't try hard with anyone. And neither do they.

The performance problem

Scroll through any dating app and you'll see it: everyone is performing a curated version of themselves. The best photo. The wittiest line. The most aspirational lifestyle.

There's nothing wrong with putting your best foot forward. But when every profile is a performance, the whole experience becomes theatre. You're not meeting people — you're auditioning.

And audiences don't fall in love.

What we're trying to fix

We started building Found because we wanted something different. Not another swipe deck. Not another inbox full of "hey." Not another algorithm that rewards attractive strangers over compatible ones.

Our idea was simple and maybe a little stubborn: what if an app could notice when someone compatible was actually near you — in real life — and quietly help you notice them too?

No swiping. No endless profiles. No performing for strangers.

Just a quiet nudge when the right person is in the same room.

Meeting in real life isn't broken. We just stopped trying.

The truth is, humans are still wired for the cafe. The bookshop. The waiting-for-the-ferry moment. We still remember how it feels when someone's eyes catch yours and something happens that no algorithm can replicate.

Dating apps didn't ruin romance. They just replaced the part where we had to be brave.

Found is our attempt to give that back.

We're launching in Sydney first

We're starting in Manly because it's where we live, and because we think small coastal suburbs are the perfect testing ground for an app that's supposed to help you meet people in real places, not through screens.

If you're tired of the apps, if you believe there's still something real worth finding — join the waitlist. Your first match is free.
